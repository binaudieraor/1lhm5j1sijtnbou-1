<p><p>The craft beer market is primarily segmented into ales and lagers, each with distinct characteristics. Ales are fermented at warmer temperatures with top-fermenting yeast, resulting in robust flavors and aromas, often highlighting fruity and floral notes. Lagers, in contrast, are brewed with bottom-fermenting yeast at cooler temperatures, leading to a cleaner, crisper taste profile. Both segments cater to diverse consumer preferences, contributing to the rising demand for craft beers as enthusiasts seek unique and innovative brewing styles.</p></p>
