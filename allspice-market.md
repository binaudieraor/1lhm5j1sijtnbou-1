<p><p>The allspice market encompasses three main types: ground allspice, whole allspice, and rub allspice. Ground allspice is finely powdered and commonly used in baked goods and savory dishes, providing convenience and intense flavor. Whole allspice, featuring dried berry seeds, is favored for its aromatic qualities and longer shelf life, used in pickling and slow-cooked recipes. Rub allspice typically combines allspice with various herbs and spices, ideal for marinating meats, enhancing flavor profiles in barbecue and grilling applications.</p></p>
