Additionally, the rising prevalence of lactose intolerance and gluten allergies among consumers has necessitated the shift toward alternative protein sources. The food industry is also innovating with soy flakes, incorporating them into new and diverse culinary applications. Sustainability concerns continue to influence purchasing decisions, as soy is often viewed as a more environmentally friendly protein option compared to animal-based sources. <p><p>The soy flakes market is divided into two primary types: organic and conventional. Organic soy flakes are produced from non-GMO soybeans cultivated without synthetic pesticides or fertilizers, appealing to health-conscious consumers and those seeking sustainable products. Conventional soy flakes, on the other hand, are derived from genetically modified soybeans and may involve the use of chemical inputs, making them generally more affordable. Both types are used in various culinary applications, providing plant-based protein and nutrition.</p></p>
